Akwa Ibom, Nigeria

Nigeria is a diverse and vibrant country located in West Africa. It is the most populous country on the continent, with over 200 million people, and is known for its rich cultural heritage, comprising over 250 ethnic groups, including the Hausa, Yoruba, and Igbo. The country has a mixed economy, with oil and gas being major contributors to its GDP, but agriculture also plays a significant role. Nigeria is often referred to as the "Giant of Africa" due to its size and economic potential. Culturally, Nigeria is renowned for its music, film (notably Nollywood), literature, and art. The country has produced globally recognized figures, including writers like Chinua Achebe and Wole Soyinka.
